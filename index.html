Occupational Health & Safety Administration (OSHA) Electric Safety Standards

By Sinenhlanhla Magubane

OSHA Standard 1926.431 Overview

  • Only trained professionals may work on electrical systems.
  • All tools and machines must be properly maintained and stored.
  • Includes safety practices like:
    • Explosion Proofing
    • Dust Proofing
    • Maintenance & Upkeep
    • Reporting & Repair
      •

Hazards

Electric Shock

Occurs when a person becomes part of a circuit. Do not touch the person, switch off the main power immediately.

Jewelry Risks

Jewelry is an electric conductor. Remove all metal accessories and wear insulated clothing and boots.

Faulty Cables

Never handle damaged cables. Contact Eskom at 033 123 1234 or email: eskom@office.co.za. Use insulated tools if urgent.

Overhead Hanging Cables

Stay away from hanging wires. Do not touch or hang on anything. Report to Eskom immediately if cables are 3 meters from ground.

Safety Tips

  • DO NOT attempt electrical repairs yourself.
  • ALWAYS wear insulated gloves and shoes.
  • KNOW where the circuits are, and how to shut them down.
  • REPORT AND REPAIR damaged wires immediately.
